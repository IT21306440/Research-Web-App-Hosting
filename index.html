Solar Smart

Who are Solar Smart?

Welcome to Solar Smart, a forward-thinking initiative dedicated to supporting national energy transformation through AI-driven innovation. Our integrated platform empowers the Ceylon Electricity Board with advanced tools for solar irradiance forecasting, segmented demand prediction, optimized battery storage, predictive maintenance, and intelligent site selection. Designed to enhance grid reliability, reduce operational costs, and align with Sri Lanka’s renewable energy goals, our system delivers real-time, data-backed insights for strategic energy planning. Together, we can shape a smarter, more sustainable energy future.

Our Domain

  The rapid rise in solar energy adoption has introduced new challenges in ensuring efficient generation, distribution, and maintenance of power systems. With increasing amounts of data from solar plants, weather stations, and energy grids, traditional energy management methods are struggling to keep up with the complexity and scale of operations.

This is where artificial intelligence (AI), machine learning (ML), and deep learning (DL) come into play. These technologies offer advanced capabilities in forecasting solar irradiance, predicting energy demand, optimizing battery storage, and automating solar panel maintenance. By analyzing complex datasets and learning patterns over time, AI-based systems can make faster and more accurate decisions than manual methods.

Integrating these smart solutions into energy systems not only improves reliability and efficiency but also supports long-term goals for sustainability, cost savings, and national energy security. As Sri Lanka pushes toward renewable energy targets, data-driven innovation becomes essential for powering a smarter future.

  Although AI is transforming solar energy systems, there are still important challenges to overcome. Many existing tools cannot accurately predict solar energy levels because they don’t fully consider seasonal changes or sudden weather shifts. Likewise, current energy demand predictions often ignore real-world factors like population growth or economic activity, making them less reliable.

Battery systems are also a concern. Most still use fixed rules and can’t adapt in real time, which leads to wasted energy and shorter battery life. When it comes to solar panel maintenance, many systems fail to properly combine data from images, sensors, and repair logs. Lastly, some AI models make decisions that are hard to explain, which can be risky in national energy planning.

Our research aims to fix these gaps by building smarter, more flexible forecasting models, improving battery management with real-time decision-making, and adding explainable AI so that system outputs are easier to understand and trust.

  Many solar irradiance forecasting models lack accuracy due to limited consideration of seasonal shifts and real-time weather anomalies.

  Traditional demand forecasting models fail to integrate socio-economic indicators, leading to unreliable long-term energy predictions.

  Static battery control strategies are inefficient in adapting to real-time energy cost fluctuations and solar output variability.

  Maintenance systems struggle to fuse data from thermal images, sensor logs, and maintenance records for accurate fault detection.

  Many AI models used in solar optimization lack transparency, making their predictions difficult to interpret and trust in operational settings.

  Develop advanced machine learning models (e.g., LSTM, XGBoost, PatchTST) to accurately forecast solar irradiance under varying weather and seasonal conditions.

  Build a hybrid forecasting model that integrates economic, demographic, and climatic data to predict energy demand across different time segments (peak, off-peak, daytime).

  Design and evaluate optimization algorithms (GA, PSO, DDPG, MORL) for intelligent battery charge-discharge scheduling to improve energy efficiency and reduce costs.

  Create a predictive maintenance system using thermal image analysis and time-series sensor data to detect faults and reduce downtime in solar power systems.

  Incorporate explainable AI (XAI) techniques such as SHAP and LIME to make model decisions transparent, interpretable, and trustworthy for real-world energy applications.

What do we provide?

Solar Irradiance Forecasting



Predict solar power generation using multivariate time series models. Our hybrid LSTM and XGBoost models offer accurate short- and long-term forecasts by analyzing weather patterns, seasonal changes, and historical irradiance data.

Energy Demand Forecasting



Forecast energy consumption across peak, off-peak, and daytime periods using AI models. Our system combines machine learning with socio-economic indicators for better alignment between solar supply and national grid demand.

Smart Battery Optimization



Optimize energy storage with real-time decision-making using GA, PSO, and reinforcement learning. This system improves battery life, stabilizes grid operations, and reduces costs by adapting to dynamic market and weather conditions.

Predictive Maintenance



Detect faults in solar infrastructure using AI-powered image analysis and sensor data. Our system helps reduce downtime and repair costs by identifying dust, thermal anomalies, and inverter faults before they escalate.

Tools and Technologies

Python



Used as the core programming language for data preprocessing, modeling, dashboard logic, and integration of all modules.

TensorFlow



Utilized to build deep learning models for solar irradiance forecasting and inverter fault detection using LSTM and autoencoders.

PyTorch



Applied in transformer-based models like PatchTST and Vision Transformers for thermal anomaly detection and time-series forecasting.

XGBoost



Used for hybrid modeling with LSTM to improve solar irradiance and demand forecasting accuracy.

OpenAI Gym



Used to simulate environments for reinforcement learning-based battery storage optimization (DDPG, MORL).

Scikit-learn



Used for preprocessing, feature selection, Random Forest modeling, and Isolation Forest-based anomaly detection.

Azure SQL



Cloud-based database for storing preprocessed data, model outputs, cost records, and solar metadata across modules.

Plotly Dash



Used to create the interactive dashboard that integrates all modules for CEB decision-making and visualization.
